Day 0 · Sunday, April 5
Arrive & Settle In
Jet lag day. Get Suica cards, check in, eat something easy. Don't over-plan.
Airport → Hotel
Narita Express (N'EX) to Tokyo
Load Suica cards at the airport — you'll tap in and out all week on trains, subway, and even convenience stores. The N'EX is a great first transport experience: reserved seats, luggage racks, scenic run into the city. From Tokyo Station, take the Ginza Line one stop to Ginza.
N'EX trainSuica cards
Afternoon
Check in — Hyatt Centric Ginza
Drop bags, freshen up. Ginza is right outside — walk the main boulevard (closed to cars Sunday afternoons). Browse department store basement food halls (depachika) for safe GF snacks to keep in the room all week.

Dinner
🍽 Oh Nana! — 100% GF Japanese Café
A 15-min train ride to Kanda (Chiyoda, near Kanda station). Owner Nana is celiac herself — everything is made safely with no cross-contamination. Comfort Japanese food: rice dishes, soups, mains. Open Sundays 11:30am–10pm. A perfect low-effort first night.
dinner100% GF · celiac safe
📍 Ginza Line to Nihonbashi, change to Sobu for Kanda. Or taxi ~15 min.
Day 1 · Monday, April 6
Tsukiji + Harajuku + Udon
Street food morning, soufflé pancakes in the afternoon, GF udon for dinner. Covers two great neighborhoods at an easy pace.
9:00 – 11:30 am
🦐 Tsukiji Outer Market
Most stalls close by 1pm so 9am is still plenty early. Graze your way through: fresh sushi, tamagoyaki egg rolls, grilled scallops, uni on rice, oysters, tuna skewers. Most fresh seafood is naturally GF. Easy walk (~15 min) or one stop on the Hibiya Line from Ginza.
street foodmarket
⚠ Ask about soy sauce on any prepared or sauced items. Request グルテンフリー (gluten-free) or plain.
12:00 – 1:30 pm
🥞 Uzna Omom — GF Soufflé Pancakes
Head to Harajuku / Cat Street (30 min on the Hibiya Line to Meiji-Jingumae). Opens 11am so no rush — a lunchtime arrival works perfectly. Rice flour soufflé pancakes, famously jiggly. Also try the earl grey yogurt. Open daily including Mondays.
pancakesGF rice flour🥞
📍 Hidden on a side street off Cat Street, near Harajuku. Address: 5-17-8 Jingumae, Shibuya.
2:00 – 5:00 pm
Cat Street + Harajuku backstreets
Browse the quiet backstreets of Ura-Harajuku — independent fashion, vintage, and lifestyle shops. Far less chaotic than Takeshita (saving that for Wednesday). Optional detour north to Ueno Station for Avan's GF rice-flour churros (inside JR paid area — tap in with Suica).

🍜 Esoragoto Udon — GF Udon
Dedicated GF udon — a genuine rarity in Japan. Confirm current hours and book ahead if possible; small restaurant. Deeply satisfying dinner after a big food day. Check their Instagram for current location and hours.

🍡 Mochi-Making Class

Shibuya Crossing
Walk the famous scramble crossing — best to be in the middle of the crowd, not just watching. The Starbucks above has a window view if you want to watch. Consider Shibuya Sky rooftop observation deck (book online) — one of the best panoramas in Tokyo, great for teens.
sightseeing
Mid-afternoon
Nakameguro Canal Walk + I'm Donut?
10-min walk from Shibuya. The Meguro River canal is one of Tokyo's most beautiful walks — late cherry blossoms or petals on the water in early April. I'm Donut? Nakameguro is right on the canal: impossibly soft, cloud-like donuts. Ask specifically for GF option at the counter.
canal walkdonuts
⚠ Not all flavors at I'm Donut? are GF — confirm the GF option specifically when ordering.
Dinner
🍜 Shinbu Sakiya — GF Ramen
Dedicated GF ramen in Shibuya's Dogenzaka area. Order at the machine outside (English available), then head in. The double-meat ramen and GF fried chicken are the standouts. GF gyoza too. Open until 4:30am — no need to rush.
dinnerGF · dedicated
📍 2-10-3 Dogenzaka, Shibuya. Near Shibuya Station, Dogenzaka exit.

🥞 Rizlabo Kitchen — GF Soufflé Pancakes
Opens at noon. Time your exit from the cat café to arrive right at opening — it fills fast. Rizlabo is a 5-min walk from Harajuku Station and literally 2 min from Takeshita Street. All GF, beautiful rice flour soufflés. Budget 20 min wait for pancakes to cook. Also serves savory dishes and drinks.

🍱 T's Kitchen — GF Fried Food
15 min from Harajuku to Roppongi. T's Kitchen is 2F — look for the sign at street level. Chicken katsu, gyoza, takoyaki, orange chicken, spring rolls — all GF. Closed Tuesdays. Open lunch + dinner; time to eat before heading to Toyosu.

Day 4 · Thursday, April 9
Hakone Day Trip
The ultimate transport day: Romancecar → mountain switchback train → ropeway → lake cruise → Romancecar back. Evening splurge dinner in Asakusa.
9:00 am — Shinjuku
🚄 Odakyu Romancecar to Hakone
Take the Ginza Line from Ginza to Shinjuku (8 min). Board the Romancecar — book reserved seats in advance at odakyu.jp, ideally panoramic front seats. ~90 min scenic journey to Hakone-Yumoto. No changes needed. Arriving ~10:30am still gives you a full day on the loop.
Romancecar 🚄book in advance
🎟 Buy the Hakone Freepass at Shinjuku — covers Romancecar (upgrade fee), Tozan train, ropeway, lake cruise, and buses all day. Best value for the full loop.
10:30 am
🚞 Hakone Tozan Switchback Train
The steepest mountain railway in Japan. Three switchbacks to climb the mountain — the driver physically walks between ends at each switch. Incredibly fun for transport-loving families. Forested mountain scenery all the way up to Gora (~50 min). Arrive Gora ~11:20am.
switchback train 🚞scenic
11:30 am – 1:30 pm
Hakone Open-Air Museum
One stop from Gora on the funicular to Chokoku-no-Mori. Outdoor sculpture park set against mountain views — Picasso pavilion, a stained-glass tower you can climb, a natural hot spring foot bath. Interactive and spacious; not a stuffy museum. Budget 1.5–2 hours.
museum · outdoor
1:30 pm
Lunch — Pack Ahead
Hakone restaurants are not GF-aware. Best strategy: pack GF onigiri and snacks from a Tokyo convenience store before you leave (FamilyMart / 7-Eleven have rice-based items). On the mountain, stick to plain grilled corn, plain rice, or fresh fruit if buying locally.
packed lunch
⚠ Do not rely on Hakone restaurants for celiac-safe food. Pack from Tokyo. Avoid anything sauced, fried, or marinaded.
3:00 pm
🚡 Hakone Ropeway → Owakudani
Gondola ride over active volcanic valleys — sulfuric steam rising from the earth below, dramatic views. Stop at Owakudani: volcanic rock landscape, steam vents, and the famous black eggs (kurotamago) — hard-boiled in the sulfur springs, turning the shells black. They're regular eggs inside, but eating one supposedly adds 7 years to your life. Naturally GF.
ropeway 🚡volcanic landscapeblack eggsGF
4:30 pm
⛵ Lake Ashi Sightseeing Cruise
Continue the ropeway down to Togendai. Board the pirate-ship-style Lake Ashi cruise — sweeping views of Mt. Fuji on clear days (early April can be beautifully clear). Cruise to Moto-Hakone or Hakone-machi (~30 min).

🍱 Tempura Asakusa SAKURA — Splurge Dinner
100% dedicated GF tempura restaurant on the 7th floor above Kaminarimon Gate — the iconic Asakusa gateway. Omakase courses with seafood and wagyu options, all fried in GF batter with GF soy sauce. Bar-style seating watching the chefs work. One of Tokyo's most celebrated celiac-safe dining experiences.

Day 5 · Friday, April 10
Asakusa + 🎂 Birthday Tea + ⚾ Baseball
Happy birthday Phoebe! Morning crepes in Asakusa, afternoon tea celebration at the hotel, evening at Tokyo Dome.
Morning
Senso-ji Temple + Nakamise Street
Walk Nakamise shopping street to Senso-ji — snap the famous Kaminarimon thunder gate photo. Browse for souvenirs: lacquerware, fans, tenugui towels, handmade goods. The temple grounds are free and beautiful in the morning before crowds build.
temple · sightseeingshopping
11:45 am — BOOK AHEAD
🥐 Fleur de Sarrasin — GF Buckwheat Galettes
A few blocks into Nishi-Asakusa from the temple. Opens Friday lunch at 11:45am. Husband and wife team who lived in France — buckwheat ground fresh daily for the galettes (crepes). 100% GF. The duck galette and camembert + walnut + honey galette are standouts. Tiny, intimate, exceptional. Book ahead — only open Friday lunch, Saturday lunch, plus evenings.
lunch100% GF · buckwheatbook ahead
📍 2-14-2 Nishiasakusa, Taito City. Open Fri/Sat 11:45am–2pm + evenings. Closed Wed/Thu.

🫖 Afternoon Tea at Hyatt Centric Ginza — Phoebe's Birthday
Back to the hotel for the birthday celebration. Contact the hotel before the trip to confirm they can prepare a dedicated celiac-safe afternoon tea spread — luxury hotels are usually very accommodating with advance notice. Ask them to note it's a birthday too. Take your time.

4:30 pm — Leave for the Dome
Head to Tokyo Dome
Marunouchi Line from Ginza to Korakuen, or Mita Line to Suidobashi (~25 min). Arrive early to explore the concourse food options. Look for naturally GF: plain edamame, grilled corn, sashimi, yakitori (confirm no wheat tare), rice dishes. The Tokyo Dome City amusement park and ferris wheel outside are fun to walk through before gates open.
Marunouchi Linestadium food
6:00 pm — BOOKED
⚾ Yomiuri Giants at Tokyo Dome
Japanese baseball culture is unlike anything in the US: synchronized fan chants section by section, umbrella dances in the 7th inning, mid-game fireworks, beer keg ladies who pour at your seat, and bento boxes everywhere. The Giants are Japan's most famous team. Don't worry about not knowing the chants — it's infectious, you'll pick them up.

Day 6 · Saturday, April 11
Kichijoji + Depart
Cat café morning in Kichijoji, GF bakery brunch, Inokashira Park, then back to Ginza for the evening flight.
9:30 – 11:00 am
🐱 Kichijōji Petit Mura — Cat Café
A beloved cat café right in Kichijoji — intimate, well-kept, and calm compared to some of the busier Tokyo cat cafés. Book a slot online in advance; typically 1-hour sessions. A lovely, unhurried way to start the last morning before heading to Where is a Dog? when it opens at 11am.
cat café 🐱book ahead
11:00 am – 1:30 pm
🐾 Where is a Dog? + Inokashira Park — Kichijoji
Chuo Line from Shinjuku to Kichijoji (15 min — a classic Tokyo commuter rail experience). "Where is a Dog?" opens at 11am: a 100% GF café and bakery decorated entirely in cat imagery, with a single hidden dog to find somewhere in the decor. Rice bread, bagels, GF baguettes, sandwiches, pasta, pizza, cupcakes. Then stroll Inokashira Park next door — beautiful lake, swan paddle boats, possible late cherry blossoms. The park is also a short walk from the Ghibli Museum exterior.
brunch100% GF · celiac safepark · lakeChuo Line
📍 2-24-9 Kichijoji Honcho, Musashino. Open Sat 11am–6pm. Closed Tuesdays. Phone: 0422-27-2812. The dog is hiding — find it!
Optional detour
Comme'N GF Bakery — Setagaya
~20 min from Kichijoji by train. Their GF baguette is reportedly the best celiac baguette in Japan: genuinely crusty outside, pillowy inside. Small park across the street to sit and eat. Worth it if you're bread lovers — skip if you'd rather head back and relax before the airport run.
