Current Position

I am a research scientist/engineer at Meta (formerly Facebook), focusing on abuse problems. I'm currently a Tech Lead on the Verification team in the Central Integrity organization. At Meta I have also worked on fake engagement, anti-scraping, and compliance. Prior to Meta I led various anti-abuse engineering and data science teams at LinkedIn.

Topics I care about from both a practical and research standpoint:

  • Fake & compromised accounts
  • Fake engagement
  • Authentication & identity verification
  • Scraping & bot detection
  • Adversarial machine learning
  • Spam
  • Measurement & experimentation for abuse

My book on Machine Learning and Security contains everything I knew about the topic as of late 2017. Some representative overview talks:

  • Enigma 2020 — Good and bad approaches to measuring abuse on the internet.
  • ScAINet 2018 — Challenges of ML for security in the real world.
  • Enigma 2016 — Approaches to protecting accounts from takeover.
  • Strata 2015 — Overview of the kinds of anti-abuse systems I work on.

Academic Background

I was previously a postdoctoral scholar in the Applied Cryptography Group at Stanford University, under the supervision of Dan Boneh. My research focused on cryptographic applications of number theory and arithmetic geometry — elliptic and hyperelliptic curve cryptography, pairing-based systems, and lattice-based cryptography.

I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics from UC Berkeley, advised by Ken Ribet and Ed Schaefer. In 2009 I was an NSF postdoctoral fellow at CWI, Amsterdam and Universiteit Leiden under Ronald Cramer.

Other works in progress (v1.0 released December 26, 2010; v2.0 released April 10, 2014).
